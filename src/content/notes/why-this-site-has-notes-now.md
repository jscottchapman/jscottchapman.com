I rebuilt this site so I could publish more than a single page.

For years it was one static file: who I am, a few case studies, a way to reach me. That's still the front door. But the work I do keeps generating things worth writing down: a pattern that finally clicked, a tool I stopped fighting and started trusting, a build that went sideways in an instructive way. None of that fit on a homepage.

So: **notes**. Short pieces, written when I have something to say rather than on a schedule. Mostly about building software end to end, staying close to the people using it, and the AI tooling I'm actually putting into production, not the demo-day version.

If that's your kind of thing, there's a way to follow along at the bottom of the page. Otherwise, read what's useful and ignore the rest. That's the deal.
